Sidr

Sidr is a delicious winter fruit, and is mentioned in the Quran as “Sidr”. Sidr has many health benefits, as it is rich in fiber, vitamins, and elements that are beneficial to the health of the body. Dr. Sayed Hammad, Professor of Nutrition at the National Institute of Nutrition, said that Sidr is a good fruit that is suitable for diabetics and immune system patients, in addition to its many benefits in helping the body get rid of influenza because it contains sources of vitamins and minerals that are important for the body, but it should not be consumed excessively so as not to cause harm to the body.

Watermelon

Watermelon is a type of melon that belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family. It is a summer climbing plant that produces spherical or cylindrical fruits that are light green or dark green in color, with a sweet-tasting red pulp. Other varieties are produced from it with different pulp colors, such as yellow, orange, and white, in which black seeds are spread with white pulp. Watermelon analysis found that 100 grams of it contains: 90 grams of water, 10 grams of sugar, about half a gram of protein, 7 milligrams of fat, about 9 milligrams of calcium, 5 milligrams of vitamin A, 10 milligrams of vitamin C, about 30 milligrams of iron, 3 milligrams of sodium, 20 milligrams of magnesium, 15 milligrams of phosphorus, and about 15 milligrams of potassium. 100 grams also give the body about 50 calories. Watermelon seeds are rich in highly beneficial proteins, amounting to 30 milligrams in (10 grams) of seeds, with a high percentage of unsaturated fats that are nutritionally beneficial, especially the one called (Omega), which helps in reducing cholesterol levels in the blood. Watermelon fruit is used as a stimulating and hydrating food, and various jams are made from it. Watermelon fruits ripen within (3-4) months from the start of planting the seeds. When tapped, the ripe fruit makes a muffled sound, while when unripe, the sound is resonant.

Vegetables

Vegetables is a term used for any type of herbaceous plant that is used partially or completely in cooking to prepare food for humans. Mushrooms are also included among vegetables, although they are not considered plants, but fungi. Vegetables can be independent parts of plants, such as leaves (lettuce, cabbage, grape leaves (grapes are considered fruits)), the stem of the plant (asparagus), the root (carrots or beets), bulbs (garlic and onions), or seeds (beans and peas). And of course, fruits in many cases, such as peppers, cucumbers, pumpkins, and squash.
